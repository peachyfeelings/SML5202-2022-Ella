History

The Origins of Madrid

Madrid was a small settlement in 9th century called Magerit, controlled by Moors. In 1083, it was conquered by King Alfonso VI of Castile and became the capital of the Castile region.

Medieval Madrid

During the Middle Ages, Madrid was a small town with a population of around 5,000. It was surrounded by walls and its main industry was agriculture. The city's most important building was the Royal Palace, built in the 14th century.

The so called "Golden Age" of Madeid

In the 16th century, Madrid experienced a period of prosperity and growth known as the Golden Age. This was a time of great cultural and artistic achievements, as well as political and economic stability.

Madrid in the Modern Era

In the 19th century, Madrid began to modernise and industrialise. The city's population grew rapidly and new neighborhoods and buildings were constructed.

The 20th century and the present

In the 20th century, Madrid saw an even bigger amount of development and modernisation. The Spanish Civil War of the 1930s had a major impact on the city, but it has since become a major tourist destination and hub of Spain.
